Everything SVCAF does flows from three core commitments in our bylaws. Our programs are organized around each one.

---

## Mission 1: Educate

> *Providing education to Chinese communities on the legal and political systems in California and the nation.*

### 🎓 Education & Policy Seminars

We research and publish analysis on legislation, court cases, and policies that affect the Chinese American community — then share findings through public seminars, webinars, and written reports.

**Highlights:**
- [AI4Legislation Seminar Series](/posts/call-for-volunteers-for-ai4legislation-using-ai-to-enhance-civic-awareness-and-action/) — using AI tools to track and understand bills
- [OpenClaw AI Seminar](/posts/svcaf-openclaw-seminar-recap-ai-agents-automation-whats-possible/) — how AI agents can amplify civic engagement
- Policy briefs on affirmative action, election integrity, census proposals, and more

---

## Mission 2: Engage

> *Encouraging active civil engagement and political participation by Chinese communities.*

### 🏛️ Civic Action & Advocacy

We mobilize the community to participate in elections, public hearings, and legal processes — from voter registration drives to filing amicus briefs in landmark cases.

**Highlights:**
- Supported [SFFA v. Harvard](/posts/sffa-vs-harvard-forum/) — filed amicus briefs, co-hosted community forums
- [Election Integrity Project](/posts/svcaf-calls-for-donations-to-election-integrity-project-californiaeipca/) — monitoring ballot procedures in California
- Opposed [ACA-5](/posts/aca-5-is-a-wolf-in-sheeps-clothing/) and other racially discriminatory ballot measures
- Voter registration and ballot-protection guides for the community

### 🤖 AI4Legislation

A community initiative leveraging AI to help citizens track, understand, and engage with legislation in real time.

[Learn more →](/posts/civic-ai-competition-ai4legislation/)
